# Receipt Sync Landing Page Design Direction

## Approach 1

**Theme Name:** Precision Flow

**Very Brief Intro:** A premium SaaS narrative that turns receipt capture into a visible, continuous workflow from phone to structured data. It preserves Receipt Sync's bold purple hero while making the white sections feel calmer, clearer, and more trustworthy.

**Probability:** 0.04

## Approach 2

**Theme Name:** Financial Command Center

**Probability:** 0.07

## Chosen Approach: Precision Flow

### Design Movement

The design draws from **Swiss information design blended with contemporary product-led SaaS presentation**. Typography, spacing, and directional motifs make the capture-to-sync sequence immediately understandable without turning the page into a dashboard or infographic.

### Core Principles

1. **Show the transformation:** Every major section should reinforce how an unstructured receipt becomes organized, usable financial data.
2. **One dominant conversion path:** Web sign-up is the primary action; mobile downloads remain credible supporting options.
3. **Proof before persuasion:** Product UI, workflow evidence, privacy reassurance, and pricing clarity appear before the final sales push.
4. **Controlled energy:** Pink and violet accents carry motion and emphasis, while white and pale lavender surfaces provide financial-product credibility.

### Color Philosophy

The existing deep navy-purple hero communicates confidence and preserves brand recognition. Vivid pink is reserved for decisive conversion moments and active states, not decorative saturation. The white body is softened with pale lavender fields and ink-blue typography to make a long page feel calm, trustworthy, and easy to scan. Google green appears only where it communicates successful sync or verified extraction.

### Layout Paradigm

The page follows a **narrative ribbon** rather than a stack of interchangeable centered feature blocks. The workflow moves horizontally on desktop and vertically on mobile. Subsequent sections alternate asymmetrical image-and-copy compositions, compact outcome cards, and full-width product bands. This creates a deliberate reading sequence: understand, believe, identify, trust, choose, act.

### Signature Elements

- A dotted violet flow line connects capture, extraction, and sync.
- Small numbered orbit badges identify stages and guide the eye.
- Pale lavender product stages frame realistic UI without competing with it.

### Interaction Philosophy

Interactions should confirm intent quickly. Buttons compress slightly on press, cards lift subtly on hover, audience tabs change content without navigating away, and FAQ rows open with restrained motion. The experience should feel responsive and precise rather than theatrical.

### Animation

Entrance motion uses opacity and a short upward transform with 40–60 ms staggering for related items. Product cards use 180–220 ms snappy easing. The workflow connection subtly activates as the section enters view. No animation exceeds 300 ms, and all non-essential movement is removed when reduced-motion preferences are enabled.

### Typography System

**Manrope** is used for display and interface hierarchy because its geometric forms feel modern without becoming anonymous. **DM Sans** is used for body copy and controls for warm readability. Headlines use tight tracking and weights from 700–800; body copy uses 400–500; eyebrows are uppercase, compact, and pink with generous letter spacing. The design avoids Inter and avoids excessively large centered type.

### Brand Essence

Receipt Sync turns messy receipt admin into an automatic, cross-device expense workflow for freelancers and small businesses. Personality: **precise, energetic, reassuring**.

### Brand Voice

Headlines state an outcome in plain language. Calls to action describe the next concrete step. Microcopy removes uncertainty at the moment of decision. Example lines: **“From receipt to spreadsheet—without the admin.”** and **“Scan on mobile. Manage everything on the web.”**

### Wordmark & Logo

The mark is a tilted receipt tile with a clipped top edge and one bright sync spark, rendered as a compact violet-to-pink symbol. The wordmark uses a customized Manrope ExtraBold treatment with tightened “Receipt” spacing and a distinct capital “S” in Sync.

### Signature Brand Color

**Receipt Pink — #FF2B9B.** It appears only on primary actions, active states, and the most important transformation cues.

## Implementation Guardrail

Every visual decision should answer: **Does this reinforce the precise receipt-to-data flow, or dilute it?** The existing purple hero remains recognizable; the redesigned white body carries the new conversion narrative.

## Style Decisions

- The dotted violet flow line and numbered orbit badges recur across every major section, so the page reads as one continuous capture → extract → sync ribbon rather than disconnected SaaS blocks.
- Product visuals prioritize evidence of transformation: receipt input, extracted structured fields, verification state, and synced spreadsheet or portal output.
- Receipt Pink `#FF2B9B` remains reserved for primary CTAs, active states, key stage indicators, and decisive transformation cues; lavender, navy, ink blue, and Google green carry quieter trust and product moments.
- Authentic Receipt Sync interface visuals replace unsuccessful generated placeholders so the page shows the real product and preserves brand continuity.

## Conversion Design Reframe

### Chosen Visual Direction: Outcome-led Proof Ribbon

The redesign keeps **Precision Flow** but makes the customer outcome—not the AI label—the visual anchor. The hero will lead with a single promise: a receipt becomes a reviewed Google Sheets row without manual data entry. Directly below, a compact proof ribbon will replace unsupported logo theater with concrete, product-level assurances: platform availability, editable review, direct Sheet sync, and a clear trial path.

The page will use a **proof-before-features hierarchy**. A visible three-stage receipt → extracted fields → spreadsheet route appears immediately after the hero and becomes the recurring visual language. The existing violet field, pink action color, pale lavender product surfaces, and dotted flow motif remain, but generic cards and competing CTA labels are reduced. Pricing and the final CTA will repeat the same trial language, while security reassurance sits beside the workflow rather than only in a late FAQ.

The design must feel like an originator’s product demonstration: direct, legible, and calm under scrutiny. Every section should answer one question—what happens next, why it matters, or why the visitor can trust it—before asking for the trial.

### Style Decisions

- The primary visual proof is an explicit receipt input → reviewed field set → Google Sheets row, built from meaningful product information rather than decorative finance imagery.
- A quiet dotted violet spine links the post-hero proof moments into one continuous workflow ribbon. Each section hooks into the ribbon with a precise stage label rather than behaving as an isolated SaaS module.
- The ReceiptSync mark is shown as a strong product signature in both the header and hero; pink remains an action and transformation cue, never background decoration.
