# Trust-section research

## YNAB

Source: https://www.ynab.com/

YNAB places a compact proof band immediately after the hero transition. It combines multiple proof categories rather than relying on one claim: a Trustpilot score and review count, recognizable editorial logos such as WIRED and The Wall Street Journal, an Apple “App of the Day” recognition, and an aggregate app-review score with review volume. The presentation is concise, horizontally scannable, and visually secondary to the hero CTA.

## Rocket Money

Source: https://www.rocketmoney.com/

Rocket Money establishes proof near the top of the page with a member count, a quantified savings claim, and a “Featured in” label. This pattern uses measurable adoption and outcomes as the primary trust device, then supplements them with editorial recognition. The quantified savings claim includes a visible methodology disclaimer.

## Constraint for Receipt Sync

Receipt Sync must not display publication logos, review scores, user counts, awards, or quotes unless they can be verified. If no verified press coverage is available, the section should use factual platform and product proof—availability across web, iOS, and Android; fast extraction; spreadsheet syncing; and data-security commitments—rather than imitating endorsements that do not exist.

## Receipt Sync App Store verification

Source: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/receiptsync-receipt-tracker/id6756007251

The US App Store page currently displays a 5.0 rating based on one rating. This is verifiable, but the sample is too small to function as strong social proof and should not be elevated into a prominent ratings claim. The listing does verify that Receipt Sync is an iPhone productivity app and provides an official Apple destination that may be represented with a neutral platform-availability label.

## Receipt Sync Google Play verification

Source: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.app.receipt_sync&hl=en_US

Google Play currently displays 100+ downloads. Its Data safety section states that the developer declares no data shared with third parties and no data collected. These are stronger, specific trust signals than an unsupported press strip, but they should be attributed to the Google Play listing rather than presented as an independent audit. The listing also verifies Android availability and the Google Sheets synchronization workflow.

## Chosen direction

Build a compact “Trusted where it matters” band immediately below the existing hero metrics. Lead with the user-confirmed adoption claim “Trusted by 1,000+ users,” then support it with three factual proof modules: availability on the App Store and Google Play, a Google Play data-safety statement with clear attribution, and direct Google Sheets synchronization. Avoid publication logos and the single-rating App Store score until stronger independent coverage or review volume exists.
